Hola, soy Noelia.

Noelia, born and raised in Buenos Aires, Argentina. Tango dancer, DJ and teacher with international exposure.

I currently travel around the globe, spending most of my time teaching the art of dancing Argentine Tango

I've has been dancing for 14 years. I studied intensively with maestros Facundo Perez and Juana Fuerte with whom I learned the basics of the well-known Villa Urquiza style. I continued to learn both from the best representatives of the modern history of the tango as well as the famous old milongueros. In 2006 I rose to fame after becoming World Champion of Tango Salon. After a fantastic 8-year long career in Agentina, I decided to pack up and explore the world teaching my art. I am visiting NYC and will teach a series of workshops starting next week.

Tango Start - Developmental History

2000 - 2006

Dancing has always been part of me. At family get-togethers we would often end up moving tables and chairs to the side and improvise some tango moves. Lots of our parents' friends were musicians and dinners with friends often ended up with them playing and us kids dancing.

2006 - 2010

The European Connection

In 2006 a famous tango teacher who lived in Germany, Detlef Engle, visited Argentina. I had the opportunity to dance with him at milongas and being introduced to his friends. His dance partner had just gotten injured and he was looking for a partner to tour around the world with. I flew back to Germany with Detlef and we started teaching together. My experience was transformed by his technical precision. My students' experience was transformed by my embrace. I believe tha embrace is the foundation for what tango is and adds flavor to the dance, but must be fused with clean technique and precise accuracy of the movements.

2010 - 2015

A more international exposure

These are the years of packing my life away and living off my luggage, bringing my art to the world. In Germany I had the opportunity to dance with Carlitos Sepulveda and my original plan to tour with Detleft changed when I met Carlitos, who was about to take off, destination South Korea. Our focus has been on appropriate response to boleos. Colgadas. Isolating the lead. Rebote and Elasticity. And above all, of course, Musicality.
